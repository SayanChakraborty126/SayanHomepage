About

Securing Intelligent Systems Against Adversarial Threats

I am a PhD candidate in Electrical and Computer Engineering at New York University, working under the supervision of Prof. Zhong-Ping Jiang at the Control and Networks (CAN) Lab. My research focuses on making reinforcement learning and control systems resilient to cyberattacks, specifically Denial-of-Service, sensor corruption, and communication failures.

I combine rigorous control-theoretic analysis with modern deep RL to design agents that maintain provable safety and stability guarantees even under worst-case adversarial disruptions. My work spans the full pipeline from theory to deployment ‚Äî including embedded systems on NVIDIA Jetson AGX, hardware-in-the-loop testing, and real-world autonomous driving experiments.

Previously, I earned my M.Tech from IIT Hyderabad and B.Tech from NIT Silchar. I've also worked as a Control Systems Engineer Intern at ClearMotion, developing real-time 3D motion planners for active suspension systems.
